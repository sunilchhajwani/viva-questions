# Recent Advances in Emergency Medicine for PG Students
## Comprehensive Review for Recent Advances Question Papers

## 1. CARDIOVASCULAR SYSTEM

### 1.1 Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **High-Sensitivity Troponin (hs-cTn)**: 0/1-hour and 0/2-hour algorithms for rapid rule-out/rule-in<br>• **Complete Revascularization**: PCI of non-culprit lesions during index admission<br>• **Colchicine**: Low-dose (0.5mg OD) for secondary prevention post-ACS<br>• **Intracoronary Imaging**: Routine OCT/IVUS to optimize stent expansion |
| **Key Trials** | • **COMPLETE Trial (2019)**: Complete revascularization reduced CV death/MI vs culprit-only PCI<br>• **COLCOT Trial (2019)**: Colchicine 0.5mg daily post-MI reduced ischemic events by 23%<br>• **LoDoCo2 Trial**: Colchicine benefit in chronic CAD<br>• **HiSTORIC/High-STEACS**: Validated hs-troponin rapid protocols |
| **Must Know** | • ESC 0/1h hs-cTn algorithm cutoff values<br>• Contraindications for complete revascularization (hemodynamic instability, shock)<br>• Colchicine dosing and renal adjustments (avoid if CrCl <30)<br>• Difference between STEMI, NSTEMI, UAP management timelines |
| **Good to Know** | • PCSK9 inhibitors role in acute setting (evolocumab, alirocumab)<br>• Bioresorbable vascular scaffolds current limitations<br>• Intracoronary nitroglycerin for no-reflow phenomenon |

---

### 1.2 Pulmonary Embolism (PE)

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Risk Stratification**: sPESI + RV dysfunction (Echo/CT) + Troponin for intermediate-high risk<br>• **Catheter-Directed Thrombolysis (CDT)**: Preferred over systemic thrombolysis for intermediate-high risk PE<br>• **Mechanical Thrombectomy**: Aspiration rheolytic systems (FlowTriever, AngioJet)<br>• **DOACs First-Line**: Apixaban/Rivaroxaban preferred even in cancer (except GI/GU malignancies) |
| **Key Trials** | • **PEITHO-2 Trial**: CDT reduced decompensation vs anticoagulation alone<br>• **ULTIMA Trial**: CDT reduced RV/LV ratio vs UFH alone<br>• **Hokusai-VTE Cancer**: Edoxaban non-inferior to LMWH in cancer patients<br>• **Caravaggio Trial**: Apixaban safe/effective in cancer-associated thrombosis<br>• **PEERLESS II (Ongoing)**: Mechanical thrombectomy vs standard care |
| **Must Know** | • Intermediate-High Risk PE criteria: sPESI ≥1, RV strain on imaging, positive troponin<br>• Tenecteplase dose for CDT: 4-8 mg (vs 50mg systemic)<br>• Absolute contraindications for thrombolysis<br>• DOAC choice in renal impairment |
| **Good to Know** | • Inhaled Iloprost for acute RV failure support<br>• VA-ECMO as bridge to thrombectomy in massive PE with arrest<br>• Systemic thrombolysis half-dose regimens (0.6 mg/kg alteplase) |

### 1.3 Heart Failure & Cardiogenic Shock

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **SGLT2 Inhibitors**: Empagliflozin/Dapagliflozin for HFrEF AND HFpEF; early initiation in stable hospitalized patients<br>• **SCAI Shock Stages**: A-E classification replacing "cold/wet" profiles<br>• **Impella/VA-ECMO**: Early MCS in selected cardiogenic shock<br>• **Vericiguat**: sGC stimulator for recent worsening HF |
| **Key Trials** | • **DAPA-HF (2019)**: Dapagliflozin reduced CV death/HF hospitalization in HFrEF<br>• **EMPEROR-Reduced (2020)**: Empagliflozin benefit in HFrEF<br>• **EMPEROR-Preserved (2021)**: Empagliflozin first drug to show benefit in HFpEF<br>• **IABP-SHOCK II (2012)**: No mortality benefit for routine IABP in MI-related shock<br>• **DanGer Shock (2024)**: Impella showed mortality benefit in AMI-cardiogenic shock |
| **Must Know** | • SCAI Shock Stages: A (At risk), B (Beginning), C (Classic), D (Deteriorating), E (Extremis)<br>• SGLT2i initiation criteria: hemodynamically stable, euvolemic<br>• Impella vs ECMO indications: Impella for LV unloading, ECMO for biventricular support<br>• Vasopressor/inotrope selection based on shock phenotype |
| **Good to Know** | • CardioMEMS for remote PA pressure monitoring<br>• Aficamten for obstructive HCM (emerging)<br>• Mitral TEER (transcatheter edge-to-edge repair) for functional MR |

### 1.4 Cardiac Arrest & Resuscitation

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **TTM Evolution**: Fever prevention (≤37.5°C) non-inferior to hypothermia (33°C)<br>• **ECPR (ECLS)**: Extracorporeal CPR for refractory VF/pVT in specialized centers<br>• **Double Sequential Defibrillation (DSD)**: For refractory VF after 3 failed shocks<br>• **Coronary Angiography**: Immediate only for STEMI/equivalent; delayed/selective for others |
| **Key Trials** | • **TTM-2 (2021)**: Hypothermia 33°C vs normothermia with fever prevention - no difference in mortality/neurological outcome<br>• **ARREST Trial (2020)**: ECPR showed survival benefit in refractory OHCA (39% vs 7%)<br>• **COACT Trial (2019)**: Immediate angio vs delayed in OHCA without STEMI - no difference<br>• **EMERGE Trial (2024)**: Confirmed selective angio strategy |
| **Must Know** | • Current TTM target: 32-36°C OR strict fever avoidance for 24h<br>• ECPR inclusion: witnessed arrest, <60 min CPR, age <65-70, reversible cause<br>• DSD vector configuration: AP pads + LL pads simultaneously<br>• Post-ROSC BP target: MAP ≥65 mmHg or SBP ≥90 mmHg |
| **Good to Know** | • Point-of-Care Echo during CPR pauses for reversible causes<br>• Transcutaneous pacing in asystole (generally not recommended but may be attempted)<br>• Intravenous lipid emulsion for local anesthetic toxicity arrest |

## 2. RESPIRATORY SYSTEM

### 2.1 Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS)

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Prone Positioning**: Standard for moderate-severe ARDS (P/F <150); ≥16 hours/day<br>• **Awake Proning**: In hypoxemic respiratory failure to delay/intubation<br>• **Conservative Fluid Strategy**: FACTT protocol adherence<br>• **Driving Pressure Guidance**: Target <15 cmH₂O |
| **Key Trials** | • **PROSEVA (2013)**: Prone positioning reduced mortality in severe ARDS (16% vs 32.8%)<br>• **ROSE (2019)**: Routine neuromuscular blockade not superior to light sedation<br>• **FACTT (2006)**: Conservative fluid strategy improved lung-free days<br>• **LUNG-SAFE (2016)**: Epidemiology showing under-recognition of ARDS |
| **Must Know** | • Berlin Definition: Acute onset, bilateral opacities, P/F ≤300, not fully explained by cardiac failure<br>• Severity: Mild (200-300), Moderate (100-200), Severe (<100)<br>• Prone contraindications: Unstable spine, open abdomen, pregnancy, raised ICP<br>• Plateau pressure target: <30 cmH₂O; Driving pressure: <15 cmH₂O |
| **Good to Know** | • ECCO2R (Extracorporeal CO₂ Removal) for ultra-protective ventilation<br>• Inhaled Nitric Oxide/Epoprostenol as rescue therapy<br>• Recruitment maneuvers: Evidence mixed, use selectively |

---

### 2.2 Severe Asthma & COPD Exacerbation

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **High-Flow Nasal Cannula (HFNC)**: Reduces work of breathing before NIV/intubation<br>• **Helmet NIV**: Better tolerance, reduced intubation rates<br>• **Biologics History**: Omalizumab/Mepolizumab/Benralizumab affect acute management<br>• **Early Intubation**: If NIV fails within 1-2 hours, avoid prolonged trials |
| **Key Trials** | • **FLORA Trial**: HFNC feasibility in hypercapnic respiratory failure<br>• **Various Helmet NIV Studies**: Reduced intubation vs face mask NIV<br>• **MAGNETIC Trial**: IV Magnesium sulfate benefit in severe asthma<br>• **BTS/SIGN Guidelines 2019**: Controlled oxygen therapy emphasis |
| **Must Know** | • Immediate intubation indications in asthma: Silent chest, exhaustion, altered GCS, rising CO₂<br>• NIV contraindications: Vomiting, facial trauma, inability to protect airway, hemodynamic instability<br>• COPD target SpO₂: 88-92%<br>• Asthma target SpO₂: 94-98% |
| **Good to Know** | • IV Magnesium Sulfate: 2g over 20 min for severe asthma<br>• Ketamine as induction agent (1-2 mg/kg) for bronchodilation<br>• Heliox therapy (limited availability)<br>• Aminophylline: No longer routinely recommended |

### 2.3 Pulmonary Hypertension Crisis

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Inhaled Vasodilators**: iNO or Iloprost as first-line stabilization<br>• **RV Support**: Milrinone/Dobutamine preferred inotropes<br>• **Avoid PVR-Increasing Factors**: Hypoxia, acidosis, hypercarbia, high intrathoracic pressure<br>• **Specialized PH Therapy Continuation**: Never abruptly stop oral PAH meds |
| **Key Trials** | • **Expert Consensus Guidelines**: ESC/ERS 2022 PH Guidelines<br>• **Observational Data**: Inhaled vasodilators improve acute hemodynamics |
| **Must Know** | • Precipitating factors: Infection, PE, arrhythmia, pregnancy, medication non-compliance<br>• Avoid: High-dose norepinephrine without inotrope, excessive fluids, ketamine (controversial)<br>• Preferred induction: Etomidate or high-dose fentanyl<br>• Central line placement: High risk - consider expert assistance |
| **Good to Know** | • ECMO bridge to recovery/transplant in refractory crisis<br>• Calcium channel blocker challenge testing (outpatient only)<br>• Interaction between CCBs and specific PAH medications |

## 3. NEUROLOGICAL EMERGENCIES

### 3.1 Acute Ischemic Stroke

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Extended Time Windows**: Thrombectomy up to 24 hours with perfusion imaging<br>• **Wake-up Strokes**: MRI DWI-FLAIR mismatch or CT Perfusion allows treatment<br>• **Tenecteplase**: Non-inferior/superior to Alteplase (0.25 mg/kg single bolus)<br>• **Direct to Angio**: Bypass CT in LVO-suspected cases at comprehensive centers<br>• **DAPT for Minor Stroke**: Clopidogrel + Aspirin for 21 days in TIA/minor stroke |
| **Key Trials** | • **DAWN Trial (2018)**: Thrombectomy 6-24h with clinical-core mismatch improved outcomes<br>• **DEFUSE-3 (2018)**: Thrombectomy 6-16h with perfusion mismatch benefit<br>• **WAKE-UP (2018)**: IV tPA in wake-up strokes with DWI-FLAIR mismatch<br>• **EXTEND-IA TNK Part 2 (2020)**: Tenecteplase 0.25mg/kg superior to Alteplase for reperfusion<br>• **CHANCE/POINT Trials**: DAPT benefit in minor stroke/TIA |
| **Must Know** | • LVO Screening Scales: RACE, FAST-ED, CPSSS, VAN<br>• Perfusion concepts: Core (irreversible) vs Penumbra (salvageable)<br>• BP targets: Pre-thrombolysis <185/110; Post-thrombolysis <180/105; Post-thrombectomy <140/90<br>• Door-to-needle time goal: <60 minutes; Door-to-groin: <90 minutes |
| **Good to Know** | • Remote Ischemic Conditioning (investigational)<br>• Neuroprotection agents (all failed so far)<br>• Tenecteplase dose: 0.25 mg/kg (max 25mg) single bolus |

---

### 3.2 Intracerebral Hemorrhage (ICH)

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Aggressive BP Lowering**: SBP target <140 mmHg rapidly<br>• **Specific Reversal Agents**: Idarucizumab (Dabigatran), Andexanet Alfa (Factor Xa inhibitors)<br>• **4F-PCC**: For warfarin reversal when specific agents unavailable<br>• **Minimally Invasive Surgery**: ENRICH trial supports early evacuation in select lobar hemorrhages |
| **Key Trials** | • **INTERACT2 (2013)**: Intensive BP control (SBP <140) safe, trend toward benefit<br>• **ATACH-II (2016)**: Confirmed safety of intensive BP lowering<br>• **ANNEXA-4 (2019)**: Andexanet Alfa effective hemostasis in Factor Xa inhibitor bleeding<br>• **ENRICH (2023)**: Early minimally invasive surgery improved outcomes in lobar ICH<br>• **TICH-2 (2018)**: Tranexamic acid safe but no functional benefit (unless given <3h) |
| **Must Know** | • Reversal dosing: Idarucizumab 5g IV; Andexanet Alfa 400-800mg bolus + infusion<br>• Warfarin reversal: 4F-PCC 50 units/kg + Vitamin K 10mg IV<br>• Surgical indications: Cerebellar bleed >3cm, brainstem compression, hydrocephalus<br>• SBP target: 140 mmHg (avoid <110 mmHg) |
| **Good to Know** | • Spot Sign on CTA predicts hematoma expansion<br>• ICH Score for prognosis (GCS, Age, Volume, IVH, Location)<br>• Statin continuation controversy post-ICH |

### 3.3 Status Epilepticus

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Second-Line Equivalence**: Levetiracetam, Fosphenytoin, Valproate all equivalent<br>• **Super-Refractory SE Management**: Anesthetic infusions, Ketamine, Immunotherapy<br>• **Pre-hospital Treatment**: Intranasal/Buccal Midazolam or IM Midazolam<br>• **Newer Agents**: Lacosamide emerging as alternative |
| **Key Trials** | • **ESETT (2019)**: No superiority among Levetiracetam, Fosphenytoin, Valproate for benzodiazepine-refractory SE<br>• **RAMPART (2012)**: IM Midazolam superior to IV Lorazepam in pre-hospital setting |
| **Must Know** | • Timeline: Treatment SE = ≥5 min; Established SE = ≥30 min<br>• Benzodiazepine dosing: Lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg (max 4mg); Midazolam 0.2 mg/kg IM<br>• Second-line dosing: Levetiracetam 60mg/kg (max 4.5g); Fosphenytoin 20mg PE/kg; Valproate 40mg/kg<br>• Airway management timing before third-line anesthetics |
| **Good to Know** | • Lacosamide dose: 200-400mg IV<br>• Allopregnanolone (Zuranolone) investigational<br>• Ketamine infusion for super-refractory SE (NMDA antagonist mechanism)<br>• Immunotherapy for autoimmune etiology |

## 4. TOXICOLOGY & ENVIRONMENTAL

### 4.1 Opioid Overdose

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **High-Potency Synthetic Opioids**: Fentanyl analogs require higher/repeated Naloxone doses<br>• **Xylazine Co-ingestion**: "Tranq" - causes wounds, non-opioid sedation; Naloxone ineffective for xylazine<br>• **Continuous Naloxone Infusion**: For long-acting opioids or fentanyl analogs<br>• **Take-home Naloxone**: Wider distribution policies |
| **Key Trials** | • **Observational Data**: Driving fentanyl analog management guidelines<br>• **CDC Guidelines 2022**: Updated opioid prescribing and overdose management |
| **Must Know** | • Naloxone escalation: Start 0.4mg, increase to 2mg, then 4mg for suspected fentanyl<br>• Xylazine recognition: Severe skin ulcers, bradycardia, hypotension<br>• Observation period: Minimum 2-4 hours post-reversal; longer for sustained-release<br>• Xylazine withdrawal: Treat with Alpha-2 agonists (Clonidine, Dexmedetomidine) |
| **Good to Know** | • Nalmefene: Longer-acting antagonist<br>• Intranasal Naloxone: 4mg per spray<br>• Wound care for xylazine-associated skin lesions |

---

### 4.2 Novel Anticoagulant Reversal

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Andexanet Alfa**: Specific reversal for Apixaban/Rivaroxaban<br>• **Idarucizumab**: Specific reversal for Dabigatran<br>• **Ciraparantag (PER977)**: Universal reversal agent (investigational)<br>• **4F-PCC**: Off-label use when specific agents unavailable |
| **Key Trials** | • **ANNEXA-4 (2019)**: Andexanet Alfa achieved excellent hemostasis in 79%<br>• **RE-VERSE AD (2015)**: Idarucizumab completely reversed Dabigatran |
| **Must Know** | • Andexanet Alfa dosing: 400mg or 800mg bolus + 2hr infusion based on last dose<br>• Idarucizumab: 5g IV (two 2.5g vials)<br>• Cost considerations: Andexanet expensive; PCC reasonable alternative<br>• Thrombotic risk post-reversal |
| **Good to Know** | • Activated charcoal if DOAC ingestion within 2 hours<br>• Dialysis effective only for Dabigatran<br>• Ciraparantag Phase 3 trials ongoing |

## 5. TRAUMA & CRITICAL CARE

### 5.1 Hemorrhagic Shock & Resuscitation

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Low Titer O Positive Whole Blood (LTOWB)**: Return of whole blood in trauma<br>• **Balanced Component Therapy**: 1:1:1 ratio (RBC:FFP:Platelets)<br>• **TXA Early Use**: Within 3 hours of injury<br>• **Permissive Hypotension**: SBP 80-90 mmHg until hemostasis (except TBI)<br>• **REBOA**: Zone 1 vs Zone 3 occlusion |
| **Key Trials** | • **PROPPR (2015)**: 1:1:1 vs 1:1:2 - better hemostasis, no mortality difference<br>• **CRASH-2 (2010)**: TXA reduced mortality if given <3h (1g+1g)<br>• **CRASH-3 (2019)**: TXA benefit in mild-moderate TBI<br>• **TACTICAL/COMBAT**: Military data supporting whole blood |
| **Must Know** | • MTP activation criteria: ABC score, TASH score, clinical judgment<br>• TXA timing: Greatest benefit <1 hour; harmful >3 hours<br>• Permissive hypotension contraindications: TBI, spinal cord injury, pregnancy<br>• REBOA Zone 1: Thoracic/pelvic; Zone 3: Abdominal/pelvic |
| **Good to Know** | • Fibrinogen concentrate vs Cryoprecipitate<br>• ROTEM/TEG-guided resuscitation<br>• Partial vs Complete REBOA occlusion<br>• Calcium replacement during MTP |

---

### 5.2 Sepsis & Septic Shock

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Restrictive Fluid Strategy**: CLOVERS trial supports conservative fluids<br>• **Vitamin C Protocol Debunked**: VICTAS trial showed no benefit<br>• **Angiotensin II**: Giapreza for catecholamine-refractory shock<br>• **Lactate Clearance**: Goal-directed therapy<br>• **Balanced Crystalloids**: SMART/SALT-ED suggest benefit over NS |
| **Key Trials** | • **CLOVERS (2023)**: Restrictive vs Liberal - restrictive safe<br>• **VICTAS (2021)**: Vit C, Thiamine, Hydrocortisone - no benefit<br>• **ATHOS-3 (2017)**: Angiotensin II increased MAP<br>• **SMART (2018)**: Balanced crystalloids reduced kidney injury |
| **Must Know** | • Surviving Sepsis Hour-1 Bundle: Lactate, cultures, antibiotics, fluids, pressors<br>• Initial fluid: 30 mL/kg (reassess frequently)<br>• Vasopressor sequence: NE → Vasopressin → Epi → Angiotensin II<br>• Fluid responsiveness: PLR, PPV, SVV, Echo |
| **Good to Know** | • Procalcitonin-guided antibiotic discontinuation<br>• CytoSorb hemoadsorption (mixed evidence)<br>• Hydrocortisone 200mg/day for refractory shock<br>• Albumin in cirrhotics/large volume resuscitation |

## 6. INFECTIOUS DISEASES IN ED

### 6.1 Rapid Diagnostics & Antibiotic Stewardship

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Rapid Molecular Panels**: BioFire PCR (1-8 hours)<br>• **Procalcitonin Guidance**: Reduces unnecessary antibiotics<br>• **Beta-lactam Allergy Delabeling**: ED initiatives<br>• **MALDI-TOF**: Rapid organism ID |
| **Key Trials** | • Various implementation studies showing reduced time to targeted therapy |
| **Must Know** | • BioFire panels: Respiratory, BCID, GI, Meningitis/Encephalitis<br>• Procalcitonin cutoffs: <0.25 viral; >0.5 bacterial<br>• Local antibiogram essential<br>• ESBL/CRE risk factors |
| **Good to Know** | • Phage therapy compassionate use<br>• Monoclonal antibodies for RSV/Influenza/COVID<br>• Next-gen sequencing for unknown pathogens |

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Ebola Therapeutics**: REGN-EB3 (Inmazeb) and mAb114 (Ebanga)<br>• **Enhanced Isolation Protocols**: Post-pandemic workflows<br>• **Mpox**: Tecovirimat approved<br>• **Marburg Vaccines**: In development |

## 7. PEDIATRIC EMERGENCY MEDICINE

### 7.1 Pediatric Sepsis & Shock

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Fluid Bolus Caution**: FEAST trial impact<br>• **Balanced Crystalloids**: Preference over NS<br>• **Early IO Access**: Lower threshold<br>• **POCUS in Pediatrics**: RUSH adapted |
| **Key Trials** | • **FEAST (2011)**: Fluid boluses increased mortality in African children<br>• **SPROUT**: Pediatric sepsis epidemiology |
| **Must Know** | • Age-specific vitals and shock indices<br>• Compensated vs Decompensated vs Irreversible shock<br>• IO sites: Proximal tibia, distal femur, proximal humerus<br>• Epi for cold shock; NE for warm shock |
| **Good to Know** | • Steroids in fluid-refractory shock<br>• Calcium correction with blood products<br>• Frequent glucose monitoring |

### 7.2 Bronchiolitis

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **HFNC**: Reduces intubation rates<br>• **Minimal Intervention**: NO bronchodilators, steroids, antibiotics, chest PT<br>• **Apnea Risk Stratification**: For admission<br>• **Clinical Diagnosis Only**: No routine labs/X-rays |
| **Key Trials** | • **HEATED Trial**: HFNC efficacy<br>• Multiple systematic reviews against bronchodilators/steroids |
| **Must Know** | • AAP Guidelines: Clinical diagnosis, supportive care<br>• HFNC flow: 1.5-2 L/kg/min<br>• Admission: Hypoxia, poor feeding, apnea, distress<br>• High-risk: Prematurity, CHD, immunodeficiency |
| **Good to Know** | • Hypertonic saline not recommended in ED<br>• Palivizumab prophylaxis criteria<br>• Nirsevimab (long-acting mAb) new option |

## 8. PROCEDURES & TECHNOLOGY

### 8.1 Point-of-Care Ultrasound (POCUS)

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **Lung US**: Superior to CXR for PTX, edema, consolidation<br>• **Ocular US**: ONSD for raised ICP<br>• **Gastric US**: Aspiration risk before RSI<br>• **AI Integration**: Automated interpretation emerging |
| **Key Trials** | • **BLUE Protocol**: Lung US for respiratory failure<br>• **FALLS Protocol**: Lung US in shock<br>• ONSD validation: >5.0-5.7mm suggests raised ICP |
| **Must Know** | • Artifacts: A-lines (normal), B-lines (interstitial), Lung point (PTX)<br>• ONSD: 3mm behind globe, >5.0mm adults<br>• FAST limitations: Low sensitivity without free fluid<br>• Cardiac POCUS: RV strain, effusion, LV function |
| **Good to Know** | • US-guided nerve blocks<br>• DVT 2-point compression<br>• Biliary US for cholecystitis<br>• Testicular US for torsion |

### 8.2 Regional Anesthesia in ED

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Recent Advances** | • **FICB**: Standard for hip fractures<br>• **ESP Block**: Thoracic/abdominal pain, rib fractures<br>• **SAP Block**: Rib fractures<br>• **Adductor Canal Block**: Femur fractures, knee pain<br>• **Liposomal Bupivacaine**: Up to 72h analgesia |

## EXAM TIPS FOR PG STUDENTS

### How to Answer Recent Advances Questions:

1. **Structure:** Introduction → Advances (bullets) → Trials (year+finding) → Guidelines → Future

2. **Mnemonics:**
   - **Cardio**: COMPLETE, COLCOT, DAPA-HF, TTM
   - **Neuro**: DAWN, DEFUSE, WAKE-UP, ESETT
   - **Resp**: PROSEVA, ROSE, FACTT
   - **Trauma**: CRASH, PROPPR, FEAST
   - **Sepsis**: CLOVERS, VICTAS, ATHOS, SMART

3. **Practice-Changing:**
   - hs-Troponin rapid protocols
   - Extended thrombectomy windows
   - SGLT2 inhibitors
   - Whole blood transfusion

4. **Know Numbers:**
   - Time: 0/1h troponin, 24h thrombectomy, 3h TXA
   - Doses: Colchicine 0.5mg, TNK 0.25mg/kg, TXA 1g+1g
   - Targets: BP <140 ICH, PEEP <15, plateau <30
